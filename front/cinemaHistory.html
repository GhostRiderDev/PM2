Cinema History

Silent Film Era

The silent film era, with classics like "The Birth of a Nation," marked cinema's early steps, exploring visual storytelling without spoken dialogue.

Golden Age of Hollywood

In the 1930s and 1940s, Hollywood flourished with iconic movies like "Gone with the Wind".

New Hollywood

In the 1960s and 1970s, cinema underwent a radical shift with films like "Easy Rider" and "The Godfather," challenging norms and addressing social and political issues.

Indie Film Movement

The rise of indie film in later decades provided a platform for alternative voices, with movies like "Clerks" and "Pulp Fiction" gaining public and critical attention.

Contemporary Cinema

In the digital age, cinema has evolved with technological advances and distribution changes, leading to new storytelling forms and experiences.
